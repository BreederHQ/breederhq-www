# Why BreederHQ Over Alternatives

The rest of the market is running desktop pedigree software from 2003, single-species tools with a health-records tab bolted on, or extractive listing sites with no software behind them. BreederHQ is a different category of product.

## The Two Capabilities No Competitor Has

Every marketing surface leads with **Scout AI** and **the native mobile app**. These are the two "there is no equivalent in the market" capabilities. Everything else on the wedge list would beat the incumbents on its own; these two make the competition irrelevant. Full detail in [./product.md](./product.md).

## The Full Wedge (Each Alone Would Beat Incumbents)

1. Nine species in one platform. Every direct competitor is 1 to 3 species.
2. Integrated marketplace fed by live program data. No competitor generates listings from program truth.
3. Rules-based automation across the offspring lifecycle. Offspring-born, listing-live, waitlist-opens, deposit-collected, contract-sent, protocols-scheduled, buyer-portal-provisioned. No competitor automates any link, let alone the full chain.
4. Buyer-facing surface as a first-class product (client portal with e-signatures, payment tracking, photo updates, direct messaging, document library).
5. Facilities and bookings capability with real slot-booking, capacity, and buffer mechanics across 8 categories at flat $5/mo, no per-booking cut.
6. Flat $5/mo across the entire marketplace surface, no commissions, no per-booking cut, no per-lead fee. Breeders keep what they earn; buyers pay nothing.
7. Substantive transparency layer on every listing. OFA/PennHIP/Embark/UC Davis/GenSol/Paw Print records with lab source. Allele-level genetics. Multi-gen COI. Titles + competition results with verification status. Rearing protocols completed. Weight/growth sparklines with anomaly alerts. Lethal-combination detection.
8. Audit-ready by default. Health records, vaccination logs, medication logs with withdrawal periods, milk/production records, parasite-management logs, ownership provenance, timestamped signed contracts, financial ledger, movement records, all exportable, all timestamped.
9. Embed widget syncs live listings to the breeder's own website (Squarespace, Wix, WordPress, Shopify, GoDaddy, HTML) in real time from BreederHQ as the source of truth.
10. 8-phase breeding plan lifecycle (Planning, Committed, Bred, Birthed, Weaned, Placement Started, Placement Completed, Complete) with financial rollups per plan, per litter, and per year.
11. Communications Hub unifies email and DMs with rule-based auto-replies on Pro and Enterprise.
12. Species-specific breeding cycle tools with heat tracking (Full vs Likely windows), hormone testing windows, progesterone tracking, species-appropriate gestation calendars, and dashboards for whelping, foaling, kidding, lambing, kindling, calving, queening.
13. Cross-tenant animal identity (GAID + Exchange Code) for real provenance across the network.

## How Alternatives Fall Short

### Spreadsheets and Facebook DMs

- Data scattered across files.
- No reminders, no automation, no COI.
- No client portal; buyers never see any of it.
- Everything falls apart when the operator changes.
- No audit trail.

### Legacy Desktop Pedigree Tools (Kintraks, PedigreePoint, Rabbit Register, PedFast, Breeder's Assistant, Equine Genie)

- Installed on one Windows machine.
- Do not sync between desktop and phone.
- No marketplace.
- No client portal.
- No mobile.
- No AI.

### Single-Species SaaS (Dog Breeder Pro, EasyKeeper, Everbreed, CattleMax, BreederBuddy, Breeding Better Dogs)

- Works for one species only. Multi-species operations need multiple subscriptions and multiple mental models.
- Older UX in many cases.
- No integrated marketplace with substantive transparency.
- No agentic AI.
- No serious native mobile app.

### Multi-Species Incumbents (BreederCloudPro, ZooEasy, HerdBoss, Farmbrite, FarmKeep)

- Older-generation UX.
- No integrated marketplace fed by live program data.
- No rules-based automation across the offspring lifecycle.
- No agentic AI.
- No embed widget.
- No e-signatures in most cases.
- No facilities booking.
- No buyer-facing client portal as a first-class product.

### Herd-Production Tools (AgriWebb, Cattlytics, FarmIQ, Herdwatch)

- Built for herd production, not breeding programs.
- No pedigree depth.
- No offspring marketplace.
- No buyer-facing client portal.
- Fine for a beef feedlot. Not a tool for a breeder.

### Marketplace-Only, Extractive-Broker Model (GoodDog, PuppyFind)

- Listing without the program behind it. Buyer sees a pretty page but has no visibility into actual breeding practices.
- **Extractive-broker model.** GoodDog takes a percentage cut of breeder revenue (reports place this in the 10 percent range on typical transactions) AND has started charging the buyer side too (reports place this in the 5-10 percent range). BreederHQ has verified this pricing structure against publicly filed complaints on BBB and ComplaintsBoard.
- No operational service to breeders (no program management, no health records, no contracts, no client portal).
- No meaningful vetting service to buyers (the "vetted" positioning is marketing, not systematic verification).
- The extractive-broker model is the anti-thesis of what BreederHQ builds. BreederHQ is the operating system for the breeder's actual work; the marketplace is a byproduct of that work, not a tollbooth stationed between breeder and buyer.

### Sniffspot (Adjacent, Facilities-Only)

- Dog parks only. Sniffspot's TAM is dog-park rental for dog owners.
- Takes a percentage cut on every booking.
- BreederHQ's facilities layer covers 8 categories (dog parks, equine, livestock, indoor training, boarding, wash and grooming, events and shows, specialty), flat $5/mo, no per-booking cut, with a professional-audience mix (breeders, working homes, event planners, breed clubs) Sniffspot cannot deliver.
